# Mouth of Sauron — Design (CON)

## Vision

Give the user back the choice that the feed quietly takes from them. Short-form
video is engineered to be involuntary — the scroll arrives before the decision
does. This tool moves the default: the feed is closed, and opening it is a
deliberate act, not a reflex.

The name is the whole thesis. The Mouth of Sauron was the herald that spoke the
endless words of the Dark Tower. This shuts it.

## What success feels like

- You open YouTube to watch one thing and there is no Shorts shelf daring you to
  start scrolling. The page feels quieter. You forget the feature was ever there.
- When you genuinely want Reels, turning them on is one click and zero guilt —
  the tool is a switch, not a scold.
- You never see a flash of Shorts that then disappears. It was simply never there.

## Principles

1. **Blocked by default, never punitive.** The off switch is always one click
   away and the copy never lectures. Control sits with the user.
2. **Silent and instant.** No flash of blocked content, no spinner, no toast on
   every page. The best outcome is that you forget it is running.
3. **Per-site, not all-or-nothing.** YouTube and TikTok are different decisions.
   Each platform has its own toggle.
4. **Honest and weightless.** No accounts, no tracking, no data leaving the
   browser. The only state is which sites you chose to allow.
5. **One job.** It hides short-form feeds. It does not nag, time, gamify, or
   block the rest of the web. (Those are other tools — see Samwise.)

## Aesthetic

Sauron's palette: near-black grounds, blood-red (`#c41212`) accents, bone-white
on the mark. The icon is a clenched fanged maw — a shut mouth. The TikTok block
curtain and the popup share the same dark-red language so the brand reads as one
held breath: the mouth, closed.

## Tone of copy

Spare and a little mythic, never preachy. "The Mouth is shut." "Toggle off to
pass." It should feel like a quiet ward, not a content filter scolding you.
